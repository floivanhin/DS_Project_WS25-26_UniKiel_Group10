Answer to RQ9

The data answers RQ9 with a negative relationship: teams with a lower team-average age tend to convert shots more efficiently than older teams.

The team-average ages in this dataset only span a narrow range, so the result supports a league-wide pattern rather than one exact optimal team-average age.

The quadratic model still estimates a peak, but that estimate is extrapolated when it lies outside the observed team-age range. The separate 33-year result belongs to the player-level age-band analysis, where all individual players are grouped by age and compared by `goals_per_shot`.

Key findings

  • Top efficiency: Bayern Munich (0.153), Borussia Dortmund (0.147), Bayer Leverkusen (0.143).
  • Low efficiency: St. Pauli (0.074), VfL Bochum (0.076), 1. FC Union Berlin (0.084).
  • Model note: the fitted quadratic peak lies outside the observed team-average age range, so the data does not support one exact optimal team-average age.
